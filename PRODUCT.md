# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Certified POCR peer reviewers and faculty course authors at California Community Colleges. Reviewers open the dashboard mid-workflow: they have a queue of Canvas course exports to certify against the POCR rubric, and 5 to 10 manual hours per course is the pain being removed. Faculty visit to see what the automated pass flagged before revising.

## Product Purpose

The dashboard is the front end of an AI-assisted POCR review tool. It shows every ingested course, where it sits in the pipeline (queued, extracting, analyzing, report ready), and the full 25-standard rubric alignment report with findings, affected content, and suggested fixes. Success means a reviewer opens a report and immediately knows which standards need human judgment, skipping the mechanical checks entirely.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, expensive. The product ("SONIQ") should feel like a careful senior colleague: confident conclusions, specific evidence, no alarm theatrics. One bold sans (Plus Jakarta Sans) carries everything, matching the login page; the working surfaces stay quiet and dense enough for real review work.

## Anti-references

- Purple-gradient SaaS dashboards with hero-metric cards and glowing charts.
- LMS admin panels (Canvas, Blackboard): utilitarian gray tables with no hierarchy.
- Anything that shouts "AI magic": sparkle-everything, chat bubbles as the primary surface.

## Design Principles

1. The report is the product: findings, evidence, and fixes get the space; chrome stays minimal.
2. Status is always triple-encoded: color, icon, and label, never color alone.
3. Motion conveys pipeline state and reveal order, nothing decorative beyond the aurora backdrop.
4. Every surface must hold up in both themes; dark is the default.
5. Real locations over abstractions: findings always name the module, page, and snippet.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Visible focus rings, aria labels on icon-only controls, role="img" labels on data visuals, reduced-motion alternatives for every animation, 44px touch targets, and validated colorblind-safe status palettes in both themes (checked with a CVD simulator, not by eye).
